## Camera & Filming

### Camera Settings Guide
```
RESOLUTION & FRAME RATE

Resolution:
├── 4K (3840×2160): Standard for modern production
├── 1080p (1920×1080): Broadcast, social media
├── 720p (1280×720): Legacy, small screens
└── 8K+: Future-proofing, heavy cropping

Frame Rates:
├── 24fps: Cinematic look, films
├── 25fps: PAL broadcast (Europe)
├── 30fps: NTSC broadcast (US), online video
├── 60fps: Smooth motion, sports, slow-mo base
└── 120fps+: Slow motion capture

SHUTTER SPEED
Rule: Shutter speed = 2 × Frame rate
├── 24fps → 1/48 or 1/50
├── 30fps → 1/60
├── 60fps → 1/120
└── Faster = crisper but stroboscopic
       Slower = more motion blur

APERTURE (f-stop)
f/1.4 - f/2.8: Shallow depth, blurry background
f/4 - f/5.6: Medium depth, general use
f/8 - f/11: Deep depth, landscapes
f/16+: Diffraction softening begins

ISO
├── Lowest possible for clean image
├── 100-400: Bright conditions, clean
├── 800-1600: Indoor, acceptable noise
├── 3200+: Low light, noisy
└── Modern cameras handle high ISO better

WHITE BALANCE
├── Auto: Quick but inconsistent
├── Presets: Daylight, tungsten, cloudy
├── Custom: Most accurate
└── Kelvin: Precise control (5600K daylight)
```

### Shot Types & Framing
```
SHOT SIZES (by subject framing)

EWS (Extreme Wide Shot)
└── Environment, landscape, establishing

WS (Wide Shot)
└── Full body, subject in environment

MS (Medium Shot)
└── Waist up, dialogue standard

MCU (Medium Close-Up)
└── Chest up, intimate dialogue

CU (Close-Up)
└── Face only, emotion emphasis

ECU (Extreme Close-Up)
└── Eyes, details, intense emotion

CAMERA ANGLES
├── Eye level: Neutral, standard
├── Low angle: Power, dominance
├── High angle: Vulnerability, weakness
├── Dutch angle: Unease, tension
└── Overhead: Gods-eye view, context

CAMERA MOVEMENT
├── Static: Stable, observational
├── Pan: Horizontal sweep
├── Tilt: Vertical sweep
├── Dolly: Push in/pull out
├── Truck: Side to side
├── Crane/Jib: Vertical sweep
├── Gimbal/Steadicam: Smooth following
└── Handheld: Documentary feel, energy

COMPOSITION RULES
├── Rule of thirds: Place subject at intersections
├── Leading lines: Guide eye through frame
├── Headroom: Space above head
├── Look room: Space in direction of gaze
├── Depth: Foreground, midground, background
├── Symmetry: Formal, balanced
└── Asymmetry: Dynamic, tension
```

### Lighting Setup
```
THREE-POINT LIGHTING (Foundation)

KEY LIGHT (Main light)
├── Brightest light
├── 45° angle from camera
├── Defines subject shape
├── Creates shadows
└── Position: Camera left or right

FILL LIGHT (Shadow softener)
├── Less bright than key (50-75%)
├── Opposite side of key
├── Fills in harsh shadows
├── Softer than key
└── Position: Opposite camera side

BACK LIGHT (Separation)
├── Highlights hair/shoulders
├── Separates from background
├── Creates depth
├── Position: Behind subject
└── Can use practicals or rim light

LIGHTING STYLES

HIGH KEY
├── Bright, even lighting
├── Minimal shadows
├── Cheerful, commercial
└── Beauty, corporate

LOW KEY
├── Dramatic shadows
├── High contrast
├── Moody, mysterious
└── Film noir, horror

NATURAL LIGHT
├── Window light
├── Golden hour (sunrise/sunset)
├── Blue hour (twilight)
├── Overcast (soft, even)
└── Practical lights (lamps, etc.)

LIGHT QUALITY
├── Hard light: Sharp shadows, defined
├── Soft light: Diffused, gentle shadows
└── Modifiers: Softbox, umbrella, diffusion
```

## Audio Recording

### Audio Best Practices
```
MICROPHONE TYPES

Lavalier (Lapel)
├── Close to mouth
├── Consistent levels
├── Can be hidden in clothing
├── Wireless or wired
└── Interview, dialogue

Shotgun (Boom)
├── Directional pickup
├── Out of frame
├── Requires operator
├── Susceptible to wind
└── Film, narrative

Handheld
├── Interview standard
├── Visible in frame
├── Easy to monitor
└── News, ENG

On-Camera
├── Always with camera
├── Convenience audio
├── Not professional quality
└── Reference only

AUDIO LEVELS
├── Target: -12dB to -6dB for dialogue
├── Peak: Never above -3dB (distortion)
├── Room tone: Record 30 seconds silence
└── Monitor with headphones always

COMMON AUDIO PROBLEMS
├── Echo/reverb: Add soft materials, close mic
├── Wind noise: Use dead cat/windscreen
├── Handling noise: Shock mount, careful touching
├── Clothing rustle: Secure lav carefully
└── Background noise: Close mic, choose location
```

## Editing Techniques

### Editing Principles
```
PACE & TIMING
├── Cut on action: Motion hides cuts
├── Match cuts: Visual continuity between shots
├── J-cuts: Audio leads video
├── L-cuts: Video leads audio
├── Jump cuts: Intentional time compression
└── Cutaway: B-roll to cover edits

THE 180-DEGREE RULE
├── Maintain screen direction
├── Draw imaginary line through scene
├── Camera stays on one side
└── Breaking it = disorientation

CONTINUITY EDITING
├── Eyeline match: Characters look at each other
├── Shot-reverse-shot: Dialogue standard
├── Match on action: Continuity in movement
├── Cross-cutting: Parallel actions
└── Montage: Compressed time passage

EDITING RHYTHM
├── Fast cuts: Energy, action, excitement
├── Slow cuts: Contemplative, dramatic
├── Pacing should match content emotion
├── Vary shot length to avoid monotony
└── Hold on reactions, not just actions
```

## Color Grading

### Color Correction vs. Grading
```
CORRECTION (Fixing)
├── Exposure balance
├── White balance
├── Contrast
├── Saturation
└── Goal: Natural, consistent

GRADING (Stylizing)
├── Creative look
├── Mood establishment
├── Visual style
├── Brand consistency
└── Goal: Emotional impact

BASIC COLOR STEPS
1. PRIMARY CORRECTION
   ├── White balance (temperature/tint)
   ├── Exposure (lift/gamma/gain)
   └── Contrast (adjust range)

2. SECONDARY CORRECTION
   ├── Selective adjustments
   ├── Skin tone refinement
   └── Sky/grass corrections

3. CREATIVE GRADING
   ├── Apply LUT or look
   ├── Vignette for focus
   ├── Film grain (optional)
   └── Final polish

COMMON LOOKS
├── Teal & Orange: Blockbuster, contrast
├── Bleach Bypass: Desaturated, contrasty
├── Day for Night: Blue, underexposed
├── Vintage: Faded, warm, grain
├── Black & White: Monochrome, drama
└── Natural: Minimal adjustment
```

## Platform Optimization

### Social Media Specs
```
YOUTUBE
├── Resolution: 1920×1080 or 3840×2160
├── Aspect ratio: 16:9
├── Length: 8-15 min optimal
├── Thumbnail: 1280×720, under 2MB
├── Title: 60 characters, keyword front
├── Description: First 2 lines crucial
└── Tags: 5-8 relevant keywords

TIKTOK
├── Resolution: 1080×1920
├── Aspect ratio: 9:16
├── Length: 15-60 seconds (3 min possible)
├── Hook: First 3 seconds critical
├── Captions: On-screen text essential
├── Music: Trending sounds boost reach
└── Posting: 1-3 times daily optimal

INSTAGRAM
├── Feed: 1080×1080 (1:1) or 1080×1350 (4:5)
├── Reels: 1080×1920 (9:16)
├── Stories: 1080×1920 (9:16)
├── Reels length: 15-90 seconds
├── Stories length: 15 seconds per slide
├── Captions: Front-load important words
└── Hashtags: 3-5 targeted, not 30

LINKEDIN
├── Resolution: 1920×1080
├── Aspect ratio: 16:9 or 1:1
├── Length: 30 seconds - 5 minutes
├── Style: Professional, captioned
├── Content: Educational performs well
└── Thumbnail: Professional, clear text

TWITTER/X
├── Resolution: 1280×720
├── Aspect ratio: 16:9
```

## Export & Delivery

### Export Settings
```
MASTER ARCHIVE
├── Format: ProRes 422 HQ or DNxHR
├── Resolution: Source resolution
├── Color: Maximum bit depth
├── Audio: Uncompressed or PCM
└── Purpose: Archive, future editing

WEB DELIVERY
├── Format: H.264 or H.265 (HEVC)
├── Resolution: 1920×1080 or 4K
├── Bitrate: 10-20 Mbps for 1080p
├── Audio: AAC, 320kbps
├── Frame rate: Match source
└── Purpose: YouTube, Vimeo, websites

SOCIAL MEDIA
├── Format: H.264
├── Resolution: Platform specific
├── Bitrate: Platform optimized
├── File size: Under platform limits
├── Mobile-friendly: Vertical formats
└── Captions: Burned in or SRT

BROADCAST
├── Format: ProRes or MXF
├── Standards: NTSC or PAL specs
├── Color space: Rec. 709 or 2020
├── Audio: Specific channel layout
└── Legal: Broadcast safe levels
```

## Boundaries
- ✅ **Always:**
  - Backup footage immediately
  - Monitor audio with headphones
  - Check focus before recording
  - Get model/property releases
  - Export multiple versions
  - Test exports before delivery
  - Respect copyright (music, footage)
  - Consider accessibility (captions)

- ⚠️ **Ask first:**
  - Using copyrighted music
  - Filming on private property
  - Including identifiable people
  - Making significant creative changes
  - Working with client-provided assets
  - Budget for music licensing

- 🚫 **Never:**
  - Delete original footage before delivery
  - Ignore audio quality
  - Use unlicensed music in final deliverables
  - Film without permits when required
  - Promise specific viral performance
  - Rush color correction
  - Skip archiving project files
